STUDY TIPS & SITES

5 Study Tips

1. Active Recall

Active recall is a study method proven to be effective that is almost the reverse of the traditional way of studying. Instead of reading text passively, students create questions for themselves to answer based on the topic and main ideas. As they repeatedly test themselves on the material, their brains actively recall the content.

2. Schedule Your Studying

By planning out what you will study over days or even weeks, you can digest the content easier. Reviewing your notes everyday for 15 minutes and having control over your studying schedule prepares you for longer term exams such as midterms or finals.

3. Explain the Content to Someone

When you explain the content to other individuals, you can pinpoint the topics you need to review or study more. You can make more conceptual connections while you recap the material to someone else.

4. Practice Questions

Practice questions for various topics are easily accessible online and are a great way to assess your knowledge. Record the questions you answer incorrectly so you can understand your weaknesses and work towards strengthening them.

5. Rewrite or Retype Notes

It may be tedious but rewriting or retyping notes commit them to memory and you are reviewing the topic as you do it. What you write can be used as a study sheet, too. A hack to find study sheets is to Google: [TOPIC] + CHEAT SHEET + FILETYPE:PDF. For example, if you needed a biology study sheet, search "biology cheat sheet filetype:pdf."
